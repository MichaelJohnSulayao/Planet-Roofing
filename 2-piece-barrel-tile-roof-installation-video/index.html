Why Fort Lauderdale Homeowners Choose Tile Roofing

Tile roofs are popular in Fort Lauderdale because they fit the coastal architectural style and perform well when installed with the right system underneath.

Strong Curb Appeal

2-piece barrel tile creates a premium, Mediterranean-style appearance that works well with many South Florida homes, especially near Las Olas, Victoria Park, Rio Vista, Coral Ridge, and coastal neighborhoods.

Water Shedding

The curved shape of barrel tile helps move water off the roof surface. The real waterproofing comes from the underlayment and flashings installed below the tile system.

Long-Term Value

A properly installed tile roof can improve property value, strengthen exterior protection, and reduce the risk of recurring roof issues when maintained correctly.

What Matters Most During a Tile Roof Installation

Tile roofing requires careful planning. Before installation, the roof deck, slope, underlayment, flashing conditions, roof penetrations, ventilation, and local building requirements should be reviewed.

In South Florida, it is also important to use approved materials and installation methods that are appropriate for wind, heavy rain, humidity, and hurricane season conditions.

  • Deck inspection before covering the roof
  • Proper underlayment installation
  • Correct flashing around walls, valleys, vents, and penetrations
  • Clean tile layout and alignment
  • Final inspection and workmanship review

Is a 2-piece barrel tile roof good for Fort Lauderdale homes?

Yes. A 2-piece barrel tile roof is a popular option for Fort Lauderdale homes because it offers strong curb appeal and good water-shedding performance when installed over the correct underlayment system.

What is the most important part of a tile roof installation?

The underlayment, flashing, fastening method, and deck preparation are extremely important. The tile is the visible surface, but the roof system underneath helps protect the home from leaks.
