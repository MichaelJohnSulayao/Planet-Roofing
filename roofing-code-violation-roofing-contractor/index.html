Decision Bridge

Corrective Repair vs. Full Tear-Off: How Are Roofing Code Violations Resolved?

When targeted corrective repair is the right call:

A corrective repair is the most practical solution when a physical inspection and core test confirm the underlying wood deck is completely dry, structural rafters are sound, and the violation is limited to localized items. Correcting specific metal flashing perimeters, adding required wind fasteners to shingle edges, or exposing selected fasteners for retroactive county inspections resolves the code issue quickly and avoids the high cost of a complete system tear-off.

When a complete code-compliant replacement is mandatory:

A full roof replacement is legally required under Florida Building Code Section 1511 if the unpermitted roof was installed over an existing roof (creating a third layer, which exceeds the strict legal limit of two), has active moisture saturation across more than 30% of the deck, or structural supports are rotted. Furthermore, if the system was built using materials that lack valid Florida Product Approvals for the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ), a full tear-off is the only legal way to close the violation. An honest local roofer will present physical core test findings so you understand your exact compliance options.
