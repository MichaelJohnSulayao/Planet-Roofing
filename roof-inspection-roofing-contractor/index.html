What Does a Roof Inspection Actually Cover?

A roof inspection is a systematic visual and physical assessment of every component of your roof system, going far beyond a simple surface-level check. Our inspectors examine shingles or roofing membranes, flashing at all penetrations (such as vents, skylights, and chimneys), gutters, drainage, fascia, soffits, and the structural decking condition from the attic.

In South Florida specifically, we check for wind-lifted edges, **hurricane strap visibility** in the attic, and **UV/heat-related material degradation**. We deliver a formal written condition report rather than a verbal opinion, distinguishing our deep assessments from a home inspector’s brief 10-minute surface-only roof check.

Key Triggers

When Should You Get a Roof Inspection in South Florida?

  1. 1. After a hurricane or tropical storm — Wind and debris damage is not always visible from the ground, meaning missing fasteners and lifted edges can lead to major leaks months later.
  2. 2. Before buying or selling a home — A roofing contractor’s inspection gives you granular details about the roof's true lifespan and hidden issues that a general home inspection won't catch.
  3. 3. When your roof is 10+ years old — South Florida's intense UV rays and heat accelerate aging, making an expert inspection vital to confirm whether you have **5 years left or 15**.
  4. 4. If you notice water stains on interior ceilings — By the time you see a stain, water has likely been infiltrating for weeks, damaging structural wood and insulation.
  5. 5. Before filing an insurance claim — A documented inspection report by a licensed contractor strengthens your claim, provides evidence of storm damage, and speeds up the process.
  6. 6. Annually, as routine maintenance — Catching a **$200** flashing problem early before it turns into a **$4,000** ceiling and structural repair is the smart way to manage your home.

In Fort Lauderdale’s climate — with hurricane season, year-round UV exposure, and corrosive salt air near the coast — an annual inspection is vital maintenance, not paranoia. Because Fort Lauderdale sits in a **High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ)** requiring compliance with **Florida Building Code Section 1504**, an annual check ensures your structure remains compliant and storm-ready.

Process Transparency

What Happens During a Roof Inspection — Step by Step

  1. 1. Exterior walkthrough — The inspector walks the perimeter first to assess drainage, fascia, gutters, and visible overhangs.
  2. 2. Roof surface assessment — Every section of the roof is checked for cracking, lifting, granule loss, blistering, or open seams.
  3. 3. Penetration and flashing check — All points where the roof is interrupted (vents, pipes, skylights, chimneys) are inspected for gaps or rust.
  4. 4. Attic inspection — If accessible, the attic is checked for moisture staining, mold, ventilation issues, and structural deck integrity.
  5. 5. Written condition report — A documented summary of findings, photos of problem areas, and a clear recommendation: repair, maintain, or replace.

Adds Depth

Types of Roof Inspections — Which One Do You Need?

Routine Condition Inspection

This is the most common type of inspection. It assesses overall roof health, identifies aging materials, detects early-stage issues, and outlines immediate maintenance needs. We recommend scheduling this annually or right before hurricane season begins.

Storm Damage Inspection

We conduct this specialized check immediately after a hurricane, tropical storm, or severe South Florida hail event. Our team focuses on wind uplift, missing fasteners, impact marks, and flashing displacement. The resulting documentation is essential for filing successful insurance claims.

Pre-Purchase or Pre-Sale Inspection

For home buyers, this service reveals the actual condition of the roof before closing, going far beyond a home inspector's basic surface check. For home sellers, it prevents unexpected surprises and negotiating delays during buyer due diligence.

Insurance Inspection

Florida property insurers increasingly require documented proof of roof condition for policy renewals, especially on roofs **15+ years old**. A licensed roofing contractor's detailed inspection report with photos satisfies most insurer requirements.
