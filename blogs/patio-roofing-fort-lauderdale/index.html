Roofing in Fort Lauderdale — What Makes This Area Different

Fort Lauderdale homes feature a unique blend of historic properties in neighborhoods like Victoria Park and newer, coastal residential structures along the Intracoastal Waterway. Because our city is situated directly on the Atlantic coast, roofs are exposed to high salt-air concentrations, extreme tropical heat, and severe wind-driven rain. Many local residences feature low-sloped patios, flat carport roof sections, or elevated balcony decks that require specialized roofing membranes rather than standard shingles. The combination of intense sub-tropical sun and direct ocean winds means that roofing systems here degrade much faster than they would inland, requiring expert installation.

The Most Common Roofing Issues in Fort Lauderdale

1. **Ponding Water on Low-Slope Decks:** Due to inadequate sloping, rain often gathers in standing pools on flat patio surfaces, which degrades the underlying membrane and triggers leaks. Standard systems fail to drain this weight, leading to structural sagging and rot.
2. **Failed Flashing at Wall Connections:** Water routinely leaks where the patio roof structure attaches to the main stucco walls of the house. If flashing is not properly sealed and integrated, water will seep behind the siding during heavy storms.
3. **Clogged Scuppers and Gutters:** Heavy foliage and tropical debris quickly block drainage channels, forcing rainwater to back up under the roofing material. This standing water eventually penetrates the joints, creating leaks inside your outdoor living area.
4. **Cracked and Weathered Waterproofing:** Constant exposure to high solar radiation dries out liquid membranes, leading to small cracks and tears. Once these cracks develop, moisture seeps into the concrete or wood sub-floor, creating structural mold.
