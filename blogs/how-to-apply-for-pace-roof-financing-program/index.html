How Do You Apply for the PACE Roof Financing Program in Florida?

Applying for the Property Assessed Clean Energy (PACE) program in South Florida offers homeowners an accessible way to finance a necessary roof replacement. Because PACE links financing to your home's equity rather than your personal credit score, it provides a practical solution for securing your property against severe weather. Homeowners should first verify their county’s participation, obtain a professional contractor estimate, and then submit a formal application.

Understanding the Core Value of PACE Roof Financing in South Florida

A roof replacement is a vital home improvement in South Florida, where properties must withstand high winds, heavy tropical downpours, and intense UV rays year-round. Many homeowners delay this crucial upgrade because of high upfront costs, which puts their property at risk of serious water damage and mold. The PACE program addresses this issue by allowing property owners to finance 100 percent of their roof replacement costs with no money down. Instead of regular monthly payments to a bank, the financing is repaid through an annual assessment added to your property tax bill, offering a long-term, secure path to upgrading your roof.

Financing Qualification Based on Home Equity

Unlike traditional bank loans, PACE financing is tied directly to the property rather than the individual property owner. This means the primary qualification criteria are based on the equity in your home, your property tax payment history, and the absence of active bankruptcies or involuntary liens, rather than your personal credit score.

Accessing Non-Ad-Valorem Property Tax Repayment

Because the financed amount is structured as a non-ad-valorem assessment on your property tax bill, the repayment is spread out over a flexible term of up to twenty or thirty years. This structure makes the annual payments highly manageable and can add long-term value to your estate.

Securing Essential Storm Hardening Improvements

PACE financing is restricted to specific energy-efficient upgrades and hurricane-protection improvements. In South Florida, this includes code-compliant roof replacements, wind-resistant impact windows, and reinforced structural roofing components that meet strict High Velocity Hurricane Zone standards.

Broward County and Fort Lauderdale Hurricane Code Requirements

The city of Fort Lauderdale is located inside Broward County’s High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ), which features some of the most rigorous wind-resistance regulations in the country. Under the Florida Building Code, any roof replacement must meet these high-velocity standards, often requiring premium secondary water barriers and robust fastening patterns. If an inspection shows that wind or rain damage affects more than 25 percent of a roof section, local ordinances mandate a complete system replacement. PACE financing is a highly suitable path for funding these mandatory, permit-approved structural upgrades, ensuring your property is fully compliant with local safety laws.

What to Do Next — Practical Steps to Apply for PACE

  1. Confirm Your Local County Participation: Verify that your municipality or county, such as Broward County, actively participates in a state-approved PACE program.
  2. Schedule a Certified Roof Evaluation: Contact a licensed contractor to inspect your roof’s structure, attic ventilation, and underlayment, providing a detailed scope of work.
  3. Select Approved PACE-Certified Materials: Meticulously choose wind-resistant shingles, metal panels, or concrete tiles that satisfy local homeowners association and building department codes.
  4. Acquire a Formal Project Proposal: Request a detailed, written estimate from your roofing contractor that outlines material approvals, permit fees, and engineering plans.
  5. Submit Your Formal PACE Application: Apply through an authorized local PACE program administrator to secure final project financing approvals before starting any construction.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a typical PACE-financed roof replacement cost in Fort Lauderdale?

A standard residential roof replacement financed through the PACE program in Broward County usually ranges from $9,000 to $27,000 depending on the property's size, pitch, and materials, such as asphalt shingles, custom tiles, or metal panels. This financed total covers all engineering costs, municipal permit fees, upgraded secondary underlayments, and professional wind-mitigation documentation, with no upfront cash required from the homeowner.

How long does the PACE application and approval process take?

The PACE program offers a fast and straightforward approval process, with most qualified applicants receiving approval in three to five business days once the application is submitted. Because eligibility is based on property equity and tax history rather than personal credit scores, there is no need for long bank reviews or extensive credit verification.

Can a homeowner perform a DIY roof replacement using PACE financing?

No, PACE financing regulations strictly forbid any DIY installations, requiring all improvements to be completed by registered, PACE-certified contractors who hold active state licenses. This requirement ensures that the work complies with the Florida Building Code, passes strict local engineering inspections, and maintains the safety standards of the community.

Is PACE roof financing transferable if I sell my Florida home?

Yes, because PACE financing is legally tied to the property as a tax assessment rather than the individual homeowner, the remaining balance can theoretically transfer to the new property owner upon sale. However, many traditional mortgage lenders require the assessment to be paid in full at the time of closing, so it is important to review your specific sale terms with a real estate professional.

When do the annual PACE repayment assessments begin on my tax bill?

Repayment assessments typically appear on your annual property tax bill in November of the year following your project’s completion, depending on when the county tax collector finalizes the rolls. This delay gives homeowners a comfortable window of time after the roof installation is completed before the first tax payment is due.
