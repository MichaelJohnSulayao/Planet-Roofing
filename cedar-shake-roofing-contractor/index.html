Cedar Shake Roofing and HOA Rules

If you live in a Fort Lauderdale neighborhood with an HOA, you may need special approval for cedar shake roofing. HOAs often have rules about roofing materials, roof color, roof profile and overall appearance to keep a uniform community look.

What Is Cedar Shake Roofing?

Cedar shake roofing is made from real cedar wood. The shingles, or “shakes,” are split from cedar logs, creating a textured, rustic appearance that looks different from standard asphalt shingles.

Classic Appearance

Cedar shakes offer a natural, warm, high-end look that works well for homes where curb appeal and character are important.

Shake-Style Alternatives

For some homes, composite shake or stone-coated roofing can provide a similar style with different maintenance and code advantages.

Can Cedar Shakes Handle Fort Lauderdale’s Climate?

Florida weather can be challenging because of heat, rain, moisture and humidity. Cedar shake roofs can perform when they are installed correctly and maintained well, but they normally require more care than many modern roofing systems.

  • Maintenance matters: Cleaning and sealing may be needed to help protect against moisture, moss and wood decay.
  • Ventilation matters: Proper roof ventilation helps reduce trapped moisture and heat buildup.
  • HOA and code review matters: The selected roofing material should match community rules and local permitting requirements.
  • Alternatives may be available: Composite shake, metal, tile and stone-coated options may provide a similar look with different performance benefits.

Cedar Shake Roofing FAQs

Helpful answers for Fort Lauderdale homeowners considering cedar shake roofing.

Is cedar shake roofing good for Fort Lauderdale homes?

Cedar shake roofing can work when properly installed and maintained, but South Florida’s heat, humidity and rain mean homeowners should keep up with inspections, cleaning and sealing.

Do HOAs allow cedar shake roofs?

Some HOAs allow cedar shake roofing, while others may prefer tile, metal, shingle or composite products. Always review HOA rules before selecting a roof material.

Does cedar shake roofing need maintenance?

Yes. Cedar shake roofing typically needs more maintenance than asphalt shingles, metal or tile. Cleaning, sealing and regular inspections help extend roof life.

Can I get the cedar shake look with another material?

Yes. Composite shake and stone-coated metal roofing may provide a shake-style appearance with different maintenance, warranty and performance benefits.
