Stone Coated Roofing

What Is Stone Coated Metal Roofing?

Stone coated metal roofing is a durable steel roofing system finished with stone granules. It is designed to deliver the appearance of premium roofing materials while giving the home strong protection against South Florida weather.

If you want a roof that is strong, waterproof, lightweight, attractive, and practical for Fort Lauderdale homes, stone coated metal roofing is a strong option to consider.

Best for: homeowners who want the upscale look of tile or shake with the durability and lower weight of a metal roofing system.

Benefits of Stone Coated Metal Roofing in Fort Lauderdale

Built for beauty, strength, and better long-term performance in coastal South Florida conditions.

Long Life Expectancy

Stone coated steel roofing is known for long-term durability and can last for decades when properly installed and maintained.

Strong Weather Protection

These roofing systems are designed to handle heavy rain, wind, hail, and severe weather better than many traditional systems.

Lightweight Roofing System

Stone coated metal can provide the appearance of heavier roof materials without adding the same level of weight to the structure.

Low Maintenance

Metal roofing systems generally require less routine maintenance than many shingle systems and can keep their appearance for years.

Great Curb Appeal

Choose from roof profiles that can resemble tile, shake, or shingle roofing while still using a strong metal base.

Energy & Value

A high-quality metal roof can improve the long-term value, durability, and performance of your property.

Stone Coated Metal Roofing FAQ

Common questions from Fort Lauderdale homeowners considering a metal roof upgrade.

Is stone coated metal roofing the same as regular metal roofing?

It is a type of metal roofing, but the outside surface has a stone-chip coating that gives it a more traditional roof appearance.

Can stone coated metal roofing look like tile?

Yes. Many stone coated metal systems are made to look like tile, shake, or shingle roofing while keeping the strength of steel.

Is it good for hurricane-prone areas?

Stone coated metal roofing can be a strong choice for South Florida weather when the correct product approvals, installation method, and fastening system are used.
