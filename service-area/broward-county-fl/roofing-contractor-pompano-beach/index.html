Roofing in Pompano Beach: What Local Homeowners Deal With

Pompano Beach has a mix of older concrete block homes from the 1960s–1970s and newer waterfront construction. Many properties sit close to the Intracoastal or Atlantic coast.

This unique coastal environment faces severe climate stressors—specifically salt air and wind exposure, which accelerate metal fastener corrosion, degrade roof flashing, and weaken shingle adhesion. Pompano Beach falls under Broward County’s strict High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) designation, requiring all roofing installations to comply with Florida Building Code Section 1504.

Planet Roofing frequently identifies corroded roofing accessories and storm-related wear that demand immediate repair before leading to water damage.
