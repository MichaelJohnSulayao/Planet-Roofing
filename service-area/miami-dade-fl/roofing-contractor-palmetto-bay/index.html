Local Roof Problems

Common Roof Issues in Palmetto Bay

Tree Debris and Branch Damage

Mature trees can drop leaves, branches and debris into valleys and gutters. This can slow drainage and create water backup if not maintained.

Hurricane Wind Exposure

Strong wind can loosen shingles, ridge caps, tiles and fascia. We inspect roof edges, fasteners and vulnerable details after major storms.

Tile Underlayment Failure

Tile roofs may look good from the street while the underlayment is aging below. Leaks often show near valleys, penetrations and roof-to-wall areas.

Shingle Granule Loss

Heat and UV exposure can dry out shingles and reduce life expectancy. Widespread curling or granule loss may mean replacement is the better option.

Flat Roof Drainage

Additions, patios and low-slope sections need correct slope, membrane details and drainage to prevent ponding water and interior leaks.

Decking and Ventilation

Older homes may need deck repairs or improved attic ventilation during replacement. These details help protect roof performance and code compliance.

Palmetto Bay Roofing FAQs

How much does roof replacement cost in Palmetto Bay?

Roof replacement cost depends on roof size, pitch, material, deck condition, access, permits and storm upgrades. Larger Palmetto Bay homes with complex rooflines may cost more than smaller simple roof designs.

Are roofing permits required in Palmetto Bay?

Yes. Roof replacements and many major repairs require permits through the local building department or Miami-Dade process. Planet Roofing helps coordinate roofing documentation and code-compliant installation.

What roofing materials work best for Palmetto Bay homes?

Popular options include architectural shingles, concrete tile, standing seam metal and flat/TPO systems for low-slope roof sections. The best choice depends on the roof design, budget, HOA rules, insurance needs and storm exposure.

Does Planet Roofing repair storm damage in Palmetto Bay?

Yes. Planet Roofing can inspect storm damage, roof leaks, missing shingles, broken tiles, fascia damage, flashing issues and temporary protection needs for Palmetto Bay homes.
