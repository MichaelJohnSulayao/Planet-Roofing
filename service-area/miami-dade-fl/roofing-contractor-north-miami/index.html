North Miami Roof Problems

Common Roof Issues We See in North Miami

Heavy Rain & Drainage Problems

Flat roof sections, patio additions and low-slope roofs can hold water when slope, drains or scuppers are not working properly. We inspect drainage paths and flashing details before recommending repairs.

Heat-Damaged Shingles

South Florida sun can dry, curl and weaken older shingles. If your roof is losing granules or showing widespread cracking, replacement may be a better long-term option than patching.

Tile Roof Underlayment Failure

Concrete and clay tiles may still look good while the underlayment below is failing. We check for broken tiles, loose flashing, valley wear and hidden water intrusion.

Storm and Wind Damage

High winds can lift shingles, loosen ridge caps, damage fascia and open small leak paths. After storms, a roof inspection can help document visible damage and prevent further interior issues.

Old Decking and Ventilation

Many older properties need deck repairs, improved attic ventilation or updated fastening during replacement. These details matter for code compliance and roof performance.

Insurance and Permit Concerns

Aging roofs can create insurance questions. Planet Roofing helps homeowners understand repair versus replacement options and the documentation needed for a proper roofing project.

Do I need a permit for roofing work in North Miami?

Yes. Full roof replacements and many larger repairs require permits. Planet Roofing helps coordinate permit documentation and installs roofing systems according to Florida Building Code and Miami-Dade wind requirements.

What roof systems work well in North Miami?

Common choices include architectural shingles, concrete tile, standing seam metal, TPO and modified bitumen for flat roof sections. The best option depends on roof slope, budget, HOA rules, insurance needs and storm exposure.

How much does a roof replacement cost in North Miami?

Pricing depends on square footage, roof pitch, deck condition, material selection, underlayment, access and permit requirements. Planet Roofing provides a written estimate after reviewing the roof condition and project details.
