TPO Roofing

Is TPO Roofing Right for Your Fort Lauderdale Property?

TPO roofing can be a strong choice for flat and low-slope roofs in Florida. It helps reflect sunlight, reduce heat absorption, and improve comfort inside the building. This can reduce strain on air conditioning systems during hot South Florida weather.

However, TPO must be installed correctly. If seams are not heat-welded properly, if flashings are weak, or if drainage is not handled correctly, leaks can happen during heavy rain. That is why choosing an experienced Fort Lauderdale TPO roofing contractor matters.

  • Reflective white membrane helps with energy efficiency.
  • Flexible material works well for flat roofing systems.
  • Good option for commercial and residential low-slope roofs.
  • Proper inspections help prevent leaks and extend roof life.

Durable, Energy-Efficient & Cost-Effective Roofing

Cost & Lifespan

How Much Does a TPO Roof Cost in Florida?

The cost of a TPO roof depends on roof size, roof access, insulation, deck condition, slope, drainage, penetrations, edge metal, flashings, and whether tear-off or recovery is required.

Some small TPO roof projects may fall in the lower price range, while larger commercial or complex flat roofs cost more. The best way to price it correctly is with a roof inspection and scope review.

How Long Does a TPO Roof Last in Florida?

A properly installed TPO roof can last around 15 to 30 years, depending on material quality, installation quality, weather exposure, drainage, maintenance, and timely repairs.

  • Regular inspections help extend roof life.
  • Drainage maintenance helps prevent ponding water.
  • Fast repair of punctures and open seams reduces leak risk.
  • Proper installation is the biggest factor in performance.
Code Compliant Roofing

Florida TPO Roof Building Code Considerations

Florida has strict roofing requirements because of high winds, heavy rain, intense sun, and hurricane exposure. TPO roofing must be installed using approved methods, proper fastening or attachment, compatible materials, and the correct details for flashings, edge metals, penetrations, and drains.
