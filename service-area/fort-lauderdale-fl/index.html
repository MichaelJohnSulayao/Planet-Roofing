Common Roof Problems Planet Roofing Sees in Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale's coastal environment subjects local roofs to accelerated wear that does not occur in dry or inland climates. We identify and repair these common coastal issues daily before they escalate into costly failures.

Salt Air Flashing Corrosion

Properties near the Atlantic coast or Intracoastal Waterway face rapid rust on metal fasteners, valleys, gutters, and roof vents. We utilize high-grade, corrosion-resistant metals and sealants to ensure your roof transitions remain fully waterproof and storm-ready.

Wind-Driven Rain & Valley Leaks

Heavy tropical downpours put extreme pressure on roof valleys, flashing transitions, and penetrations. Small cracks in shingle sealants or broken tiles allow water to seep behind the outer barrier, exposing the underlayment to moisture intrusion.

Tile Underlayment Degradation

Many concrete tile roofs in Fort Lauderdale are 20+ years old. While the tiles themselves are highly durable, the underlying felt waterproofing membrane breaks down over time from thermal expansion, causing dry-in failure and leaks beneath visually sound tiles.

UV & Heat Membrane Cracking

Intense South Florida sun ages roofing materials rapidly. Unprotected shingle granules flake off, and standard flat roof coatings dry out and crack, leaving flat sections vulnerable to standing water and structural ponding leaks.
