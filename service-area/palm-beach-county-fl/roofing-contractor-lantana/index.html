Local Roof Problems

Common Roof Issues in Lantana

Salt Air Corrosion

Coastal air can wear down fasteners, vents, flashing, gutters and roof accessories faster than inland properties.

High-Wind Storms

Storms test roof edges, fasteners, underlayment and flashing. Proper installation details help reduce wind vulnerability.

Heavy Rain Leaks

Wind-driven rain can enter weak valleys, vents, wall transitions and roof penetrations when details are not sealed correctly.

Flat Roof Ponding

Low-slope sections need proper membrane, slope, drains and flashing details to reduce standing water and leaks.

Tile Roof Cracking

Concrete and clay tile roofs need proper walking, fastening, underlayment and flashing details to avoid leaks and broken tiles.

UV and Heat Damage

Florida sun can age shingles, sealants, coatings and membranes. Climate-rated materials improve roof performance.

Lantana Roofing FAQs

How much does roof replacement cost in Lantana?

Roof replacement cost in Lantana depends on roof size, roof type, slope, material selection, deck condition, coastal exposure, access and permit requirements.

Are permits required for roofing in Lantana?

Yes. Roof replacements and many significant repairs require permits through the Town of Lantana or the applicable Palm Beach County jurisdiction. Work must meet Florida Building Code and inspection requirements.

What roofing systems perform best in Lantana?

Common Lantana roofing options include high-wind architectural shingles, concrete tile, clay tile, corrosion-resistant metal roofing, TPO and modified bitumen flat roofing systems. The best choice depends on roof design, exposure level and long-term maintenance goals.

Does Planet Roofing repair storm damage in Lantana?

Yes. Planet Roofing can inspect and repair storm-related roof damage including leaks, lifted shingles, broken tiles, flashing damage, flat roof membrane damage and temporary roof protection needs.
